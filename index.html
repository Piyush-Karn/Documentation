VisionX — Smart Object Detection for Safer Space Missions

By: Team BlackOps

Project Overview

VisionX is a real-time object detection system built for identifying safety-critical items aboard space stations using synthetic data.

Problem Statement: In the high-stakes environment of space missions, rapid identification of safety equipment is critical during emergencies. Traditional visual searches are time-consuming and prone to human error, especially under stress.
Real-time Detection Space Safety YOLOv8 Synthetic Data
Objective

The VisionX system aims to provide astronauts with an automated visual detection capability for three critical safety items:

Emergency Toolbox

Contains essential tools for quick repairs and system maintenance

Fire Extinguisher

Critical for containing fire emergencies in the oxygen-rich environment

Oxygen Tank

Life-support equipment for emergency breathing situations

Key Optimizations
Mosaic Augmentation

Set to 1.0 to balance between regular and mosaic-augmented images, improving detection of partially occluded objects.

Lower Momentum

Reduced from default 0.937 to 0.2 to allow quicker adaptation to the synthetic dataset's unique characteristics.

Extended Training

Increased to 100 epochs to maximize performance, achieving a peak mAP@0.5 of 0.949.

Challenges & Solutions

Technical Challenges

The model trained on synthetic data may not perform optimally on real-world images due to the domain gap.

Solution: Implemented sophisticated domain randomization techniques in the synthetic data generation pipeline, varying lighting conditions, textures, and camera parameters.

Space hardware has strict limitations on computational power and memory.

Solution: Optimized the YOLOv8s model through pruning and quantization, reducing the model size by 32% while maintaining accuracy above 90%.

Space station lighting can vary dramatically, from bright to emergency lighting scenarios.

Solution: Generated synthetic data with extreme lighting variations and implemented adaptive histogram equalization in the preprocessing pipeline.

Extended training showed signs of overfitting, with validation losses fluctuating more than training losses.

Solution: Monitored validation metrics closely and used early stopping (patience=2) to mitigate overfitting risks.
